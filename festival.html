Ganesh Chaturthi

Ganesh Chaturthi is the most celebrated festival in Maharashtra, marking the birth of Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles. People bring beautifully crafted idols of Ganpati into their homes and public pandals, worshipping them for 1 to 11 days. Diwali

Diwali, the festival of lights, is celebrated over five days and is a blend of spiritual worship and vibrant festivity. In Maharashtra, it starts with Vasu Baras and continues through Dhanteras, Narak Chaturdashi, Laxmi Pujan, and Bhai Dooj. Homes are illuminated with oil lamps (diyas), candles, and lanterns (akash kandils), symbolizing the victory of light over darkness. People burst firecrackers, exchange sweets like chakli, karanji, and anarse, and perform rituals to invite prosperity and good fortune.

Holi

Holi, known as Rang Panchami in Maharashtra, is a celebration of colors, unity, and the victory of good over evil. The night before Holi, a bonfire called Holika Dahan is lit to symbolize the burning away of negativity. The next day, people throw vibrant colors, sing traditional songs, and dance to folk music. Special foods like puran poli and thandai add flavor to the celebration, making it a festival of complete joy and community bonding
